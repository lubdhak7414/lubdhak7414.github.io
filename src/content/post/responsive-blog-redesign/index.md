## Rebuilding a blog for every screen

A blog that only works on a desktop is a blog half its readers can't use. I was asked to redesign an existing blog that had fallen behind on mobile, and to do it without losing the content or the readability that made it work. The goal: a modern, intuitive design that holds up on any screen.

> **Key Takeaways**
>
> - A mobile-first rebuild using flexible grids and CSS media queries, so the layout adapts from phone to desktop.
> - Typography and navigation were reworked for readability and easy browsing, not just to look modern.
> - Optimized images and lean code keep the redesigned blog fast on every device.

## Mobile-first layout

The first job was making the layout adapt cleanly across screen sizes. I took a mobile-first approach, designing for the phone and scaling up, since that's how most people read now. Flexible grids and CSS media queries let the layout adjust to the viewport instead of fighting it, so the reading experience stays solid whether someone's on a phone or a wide monitor.

## Readability and navigation

A redesign that looks good but reads badly isn't a win. I reworked the typography around fonts that are easy on the eyes and text that stays comfortable on small screens, with larger images and generous spacing to keep the content engaging.

Navigation got the same attention. I simplified the menu so readers can find posts without hunting, added a sticky navigation bar for quick access to categories and archives during long reads, and placed social sharing buttons that follow the scroll without getting in the way.

## Speed and the result

Fast load times were baked in: optimized images and streamlined code keep the blog quick on desktop and mobile alike. The redesign gave the blog a fresh, modern feel and a reading experience that works for everyone, which is exactly what drives more traffic and keeps readers coming back.
